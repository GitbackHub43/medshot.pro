Built by a pharmacist.

I work in a pharmacy, and over time a few things kept bothering me. People running out of meds early. Not being sure about the right strength after a change. Mixing up an old bottle with a new one. And the one that stuck with me the most — someone clearly wanting to ask a question, but just saying "it's fine" and walking away because the line is too long.

Most issues don't come from people doing something wrong. It's small things — timing, refills, keeping track of changes — that add up. And adherence matters more than people think. Not to keep you on meds longer, but the opposite. When things are taken correctly, you avoid unnecessary dose increases, extra side effects, and in some cases can even step things down.

I didn't start with the idea of building an app. I just wanted something simple — scan your label, stay organized, get reminders, and have clear information from trusted sources. No guessing. No made-up explanations. After looking at what existed, most of it felt overly complicated with a heavy push toward AI-generated answers. I didn't want that.

I'd rather keep things limited but accurate. Still building it, shaping it around what I actually see people struggle with — not what sounds good on paper.
